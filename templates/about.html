About Saudi Arabia

A kingdom of contrasts where ancient traditions meet bold innovation

The Kingdom at a Glance

Saudi Arabia, officially the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia (KSA), occupies most of the Arabian Peninsula and is the largest country in the Middle East. With Vision 2030, the kingdom is undergoing an unprecedented transformation, opening its doors to international tourism and revealing treasures that have been hidden for centuries.

From the ancient Nabataean civilization at Al-Ula to the futuristic megaproject NEOM, Saudi Arabia offers a diverse spectrum of experiences. The country boasts six UNESCO World Heritage Sites, 1,800 km of pristine Red Sea coastline, vast deserts with towering sand dunes, cool mountain retreats, and vibrant modern cities.

Travel Tips

Essential information to help you plan your perfect Saudi Arabian adventure

Best Time to Visit

October to March offers cooler temperatures (15-25°C). Avoid summer months (June-August) when temperatures can exceed 50°C in desert areas.

Dress Code

Modest clothing is recommended. Men should wear long trousers and shirts. Women should wear loose-fitting clothing covering shoulders and knees.

Currency

Saudi Riyal (SAR). Credit cards are widely accepted. ATMs are available in all cities. 1 USD ≈ 3.75 SAR.

Language

Arabic is the official language. English is widely spoken in cities, hotels, and tourist areas. Signs are typically bilingual.

Getting Around

Domestic flights connect major cities. Car rentals are popular for road trips. Uber and Careem operate in major cities. The Haramain high-speed rail connects Makkah, Jeddah, and Madinah.

Food & Drink

Try national dishes like Kabsa, Mandi, and Jareesh. Saudi coffee (Qahwa) with dates is a cultural staple. Alcohol is prohibited.
